'Likes' are a Flawed Currency

May 26th, 2013

The ‘Like’ as a concept is largely worthless. A ‘Like’ has no value, no cost for doling out. ‘Liking’ your content costs me nothing, so what’s to stop me from ‘Liking’ everything I see without considering how it affects the popularity of that content?

Like it or not (pun not initially intended), the ways in which many sites rank and suggest content is now often based heavily on ‘Likes’.

Introducing ‘Recommendations’

Recommendations on Photographer.io are akin to ‘Likes’ elsewhere, though with a subtle difference: they are finite.

10 per day

Right now every user is limited to a maximum of 10 recommendations per day. This default will probably change in time once I have a better understanding of how many are used on average.

Value in scarcity

Because you can only click ‘Recommend’ 10 times in a day, you can’t go gallavanting about throwing them hither and thither; they are to be reserved for the photos which you think other users should see.

Ranking impact

Recommendations affect the ranking of photographs and places them into the Recommended section of Photographer.io. I’m very keen to ensure new users, lesser-known users, and more obscure photographs are just as easily discovered as the most popular photos/members. A recommendation carries more weight than views or favouriting and will help other people discover the great content you found.

A subject I’ll also expand on in future is how the score of photographs (used in the rankings) diminishes over time to improve the visibility of newer content.

Fairness

You don’t have to be an incredibly active user in order to have an impact, and that’s how it should be. Popular photographs won’t be as largely dictated by how many followers or friends you have.

Recommendations vs. Favourites

Photographer.io also features a ‘Favourites’ system. Favourites are your personal way of saving the photos you love to a place where you can easily find them again. A recommendation differs in that it doesn’t necessarily have to be a photograph that you love, but more so a photograph which you believe is technically good and should be seen by other people.

Why does this matter?

It might just be me, but it feels as if ‘Likes’ have devalued a lot of content across the internet. In many places, it’s not altogther that much different from a store points card; a way of tracking what you like and using it to target adverts towards you.

I want users to have a meaningful impact on the site and for their recommendations to matter. This feature is far from complete, but I think it’s a step in the right direction.
